About

I am a mathematician working in probability theory and stochastic processes. Broadly speaking, my current research interests involve asymptotic analysis of rare events' probabilities. This is a subject of my second PhD thesis defended recently at the Université of Lausanne. During my first PhD at St. Petersburg department of V.A. Steklov's Mathematical Institute, I have studied semigroups and generators of Markov processes confined to bounded areas $D \subset \R^d$. This is a subject of my first thesis.

Current Research

Quantum Computing and Information Theory

Our recent work focuses on quantum error correction codes. Given a quantum channel $\mathcal{E}$, we study the optimal recovery operation $\mathcal{R}$ that maximizes the fidelity:

$ F(\mathcal{R} \circ \mathcal{E}) = \min_{|\psi\rangle} \langle\psi|(\mathcal{R} \circ \mathcal{E})(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)|\psi\rangle $

A key result from our latest paper shows that for any $k$-local Hamiltonian $H = \sum_{i} H_i$ acting on $n$ qubits, the ground state energy can be approximated within error $\epsilon$ in time:

$ T = O\left(\frac{n^{k+1}}{\epsilon^2} \log\left(\frac{n}{\epsilon}\right)\right) $

Algebraic Topology in Machine Learning

We investigate the topological properties of neural networks through the lens of persistent homology. For a filtration $\{X_t\}_{t\in\mathbb{R}}$, we study the $k$-th persistence diagram $\text{Dgm}_k(X)$ and its Wasserstein distance to a reference diagram:

$ W_p(\text{Dgm}_k(X), \text{Dgm}_k(Y)) = \left(\inf_{\gamma}\sum_{x\in\text{Dgm}_k(X)} \|x - \gamma(x)\|_{\infty}^p\right)^{1/p} $

This leads to our main theorem on the stability of topological features in deep networks:

Theorem 1. For any $L$-Lipschitz continuous function $f:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}^m$ and $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a neural network $\mathcal{N}$ with ReLU activations such that: $ W_{\infty}(\text{Dgm}_k(f), \text{Dgm}_k(\mathcal{N})) \leq \epsilon $ with depth $O(\log(1/\epsilon))$ and width $O(n/\epsilon^n)$.
